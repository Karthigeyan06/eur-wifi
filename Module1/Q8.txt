8. Difference Between IEEE and WFA
IEEE – Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers:

IEEE is an international, non-profit technical standards organization made up of engineers, researchers, and academics
It is responsible for creating and publishing the official technical specifications for Wi-Fi under the 802.11 standard family
IEEE defines how Wi-Fi physically works — the modulation, frequencies, frame formats, channel access methods, and security protocols at a deep technical level
Examples of IEEE work include 802.11a, 802.11n, 802.11ac, and 802.11ax
IEEE does not test or certify any products; it only defines the standards

WFA – Wi-Fi Alliance:

The Wi-Fi Alliance is a global non-profit industry consortium made up of major technology companies like Apple, Intel, Qualcomm, Cisco, and Samsung
Its primary role is to certify that Wi-Fi products from different manufacturers are interoperable with each other
WFA created the consumer-friendly branding we use today, such as Wi-Fi 4, Wi-Fi 5, Wi-Fi 6, and Wi-Fi 7
It also developed and certifies security programs like WPA, WPA2, and WPA3
A product that passes WFA testing earns the "Wi-Fi CERTIFIED" mark, assuring consumers of compatibility
