4. Basic Functionalities of a Wi-Fi Access Point

Wireless Association: Allows client devices to discover and join the Wi-Fi network by responding to probe requests and sending beacon frames
Authentication and Security: Verifies the identity of connecting clients using methods like WPA2 or WPA3, and encrypts all wireless traffic using AES encryption
SSID Broadcasting: Continuously broadcasts the network name (SSID) through beacon frames so devices can detect the network
DHCP Relay: Forwards IP address assignment requests from wireless clients to the DHCP server on the wired network
Traffic Bridging: Acts as a bridge between wireless clients and the wired LAN, forwarding frames between both sides
Channel and Frequency Management: Operates on a configured channel within the 2.4 GHz or 5 GHz band to avoid interference
Quality of Service (QoS): Prioritizes different types of traffic such as voice calls, video streams, and regular data to ensure good performance
Roaming Support: Coordinates with neighboring APs in an ESS to hand off client connections without dropping them
