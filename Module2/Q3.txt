3. Where CAPWAP fits in the OSI model, the two tunnels in CAPWAP and their purpose
CAPWAP operates at Layer 3 of the OSI model, meaning it works over IP networks. This is what makes it flexible — the AP and WLC do not need to be on the same subnet or even the same physical location, as long as they can reach each other over an IP network.
CAPWAP uses two separate tunnels between the AP and the WLC. The first is the Control Tunnel, which carries management traffic such as configuration commands, AP join messages, firmware updates, and heartbeat keepalives. This tunnel is always encrypted using DTLS to ensure secure communication.
The second is the Data Tunnel, which carries the actual wireless client traffic — the data frames sent and received by users connected to the AP. By default, this tunnel may or may not be encrypted depending on the deployment, though encryption can be enabled. Both tunnels run over UDP, with the control tunnel using port 5246 and the data tunnel using port 5247.
