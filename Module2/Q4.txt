4. What is the difference between Lightweight APs and Cloud-based APs
Lightweight APs are access points that are managed and controlled by an on-premises Wireless LAN Controller.
They have no standalone intelligence and depend entirely on the WLC for configuration, authentication policies, and management. 
The WLC must be physically present in the network, either as a hardware appliance or a virtual machine. All CAPWAP tunnels terminate at this local controller.
This model gives IT teams direct control over the infrastructure but requires capital investment in controller hardware and ongoing maintenance.
Cloud-based APs, on the other hand, are managed through a cloud-hosted management platform such as Cisco Meraki or Aruba Central. The AP still performs local data forwarding but receives its configuration and monitoring from a cloud dashboard over the internet.
There is no need for a physical on-premises controller. The management plane lives in the cloud, while data can be forwarded locally. This model is easier to deploy and scale, requires less on-site hardware, and is well-suited for distributed locations or organizations that prefer a subscription-based operational model.
