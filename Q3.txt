3. What is BSS and ESS?
BSS – Basic Service Set:

The fundamental building block of a Wi-Fi network.
Consists of one Access Point (AP) and one or more client devices connected to it.
Each BSS is identified by a BSSID (which is the MAC address of the AP).
Example: Your home router and your phone connected to it = one BSS.

ESS – Extended Service Set:

A collection of two or more BSSs connected via a Distribution System (DS) (usually Ethernet backbone).
All APs in an ESS share the same SSID, allowing clients to roam seamlessly.
Example: A university campus with multiple APs all broadcasting "Campus-WiFi" = an ESS.
