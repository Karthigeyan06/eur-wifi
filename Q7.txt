7. 2.4 GHz vs 5 GHz – Observed Differences
2.4 GHz Band:

Operates at a lower frequency, which allows the signal to travel farther and penetrate walls and obstacles more effectively
Provides lower speeds, typically ranging from 150 Mbps to 300 Mbps in real-world conditions
Has only 3 non-overlapping channels (1, 6, and 11), making it more prone to congestion
Shared with many other devices like Bluetooth gadgets, baby monitors, and microwave ovens, causing frequent interference
Best suited for IoT devices, smart home gadgets, and devices that are far from the router

5 GHz Band:

Operates at a higher frequency, providing significantly faster speeds ranging from 300 Mbps up to 1.3 Gbps or more
Shorter range compared to 2.4 GHz due to faster signal attenuation at higher frequencies
Offers many more non-overlapping channels (up to 25+), reducing congestion greatly
Supports wider channel widths up to 80 MHz or 160 MHz, enabling higher throughput
Much less interference since fewer consumer devices compete for this band
Best suited for HD video streaming, online gaming, and high-speed data transfers at close range
