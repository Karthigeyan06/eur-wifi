2. Wi-Fi Devices & GenerationsCommon Day-to-Day Devices and Their Wi-Fi Properties:


Smartphone (Modern):

Supports IEEE 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6)
Operates on both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz bands
Maximum theoretical speed up to 9.6 Gbps
Supports MU-MIMO and OFDMA


Laptop (Recent):

Supports IEEE 802.11ac or 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 5 or Wi-Fi 6)
Primarily operates on 5 GHz band
Maximum speed up to 1.3 Gbps – 3.5 Gbps
Supports Beamforming and MU-MIMO


Smart TV:

Supports IEEE 802.11ac (Wi-Fi 5)
Operates on 5 GHz for streaming
Maximum speed around 1.3 Gbps


IoT Devices (Smart Bulbs, Sensors):

Supports IEEE 802.11n (Wi-Fi 4)
Operates only on 2.4 GHz band
Maximum speed up to 150 Mbps


Wi-Fi Generations:

Wi-Fi 4 → 802.11n → up to 600 Mbps → 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz
Wi-Fi 5 → 802.11ac → up to 3.5 Gbps → 5 GHz only
Wi-Fi 6 → 802.11ax → up to 9.6 Gbps → 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz
Wi-Fi 6E → 802.11ax → up to 9.6 Gbps → adds 6 GHz band
Wi-Fi 7 → 802.11be → up to 46 Gbps → 2.4 / 5 / 6 GHz
